"Tokyo Flash" (東京フラッシュ) became Vaundy's breakthrough moment. Released in November 2019, the song surpassed 1 million views within two months of its release. This put Vaundy's name in the spotlight, helping him to get recognized in the music industry.
In a 2020 interview, Vaundy shared that he frequently listened to city pop while creating this song.
The music video for "Tokyo Flash," which was released on YouTube in September 2019, was shot late at night in Shinjuku, Ueno, Shibuya, Asakusa, and other places that evoke a sense of Tokyo. The director is MIZUNO CABBAGE, who is an old friend of Vaundy and is also active as a VFX artist.
His signature song, "Tokyo Flash," is a Vaundy-style city pop with catchy guitar riffs and mellow sounds. The music video is a one-shot video of Vaundy walking aimlessly through the streets of Tokyo late at night.
"When I made 'Tokyo Flash,' I was listening to a lot of city pop, and I listened to the songs that were selling right now and the ones that were on a lot of playlists, and I was like, 'What do they have in common?' I thought about it as I made it. Also, I think melody is more important than anything else.
The distinctive guitar riff that sounds from the beginning of this song is also a simple one that you can hum after one listen. In this day and age, many people listen to music by streaming, so if you think it's different, you'll be immediately skipped to another song. That's why it's so important to have a grip that makes you want to listen to it for more than five seconds.
"The producer of 'Tokyo Love Story' told me that he wanted to use 'Lamp' in a drama. This song is not a love song, but rather a song sung for the same generation, but when I saw the drama, I was really into it. It could be heard in the songs of each of the four main characters. In order for many people to listen to it, it may have been good to be aware that the songs can be perceived in a variety of ways.
