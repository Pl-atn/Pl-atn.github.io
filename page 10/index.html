踊り子 (Dancer)
"踊り子 (Odoriko)" is a captivating song by Japanese artist Vaundy, featured on his album "replica," released in 2020. The title translates to "Dancer" in English, setting the stage for a lyrical exploration of love and nostalgia.
Quick summary
The song delves into themes of love, nostalgia, and the longing for a second chance in relationships, capturing the emotional complexity of memories and the desire to reconnect with lost moments.
In-depth lyrics analysis
The lyrics of "踊り子 (Odoriko)" unfold like a poignant narrative, beginning with a sense of loss and reflection. The opening lines, "ねぇ、どっかに置いてきたような 事が一つ二つ浮いているけど" (Hey, it feels like I've left behind one or two things), immediately establish a tone of introspection. The narrator acknowledges the remnants of past experiences that linger in their mind, suggesting a desire to reclaim what has been lost. This theme of nostalgia is further emphasized in the lines, "ねぇ、ちゃんと拾っておこう" (Hey, let's make sure to pick them up), indicating a proactive approach to salvaging memories before they fade away.
As the song progresses, the narrator expresses a yearning for a shared future with a significant other, as seen in the repeated phrases about their future together. The lines, "回り出した　あの子と僕の未来が 止まりどっかで　またやり直せたら" (If only the future with that girl could start again somewhere), encapsulate the longing for a second chance in love. This desire is not just about rekindling a relationship but also about the hope of revisiting moments that defined their connection. The whimsical sound of the repeated "とぅるるる" (turururu) adds a playful yet bittersweet layer to the narrative, contrasting the deeper emotional themes with a lighthearted musicality.
The lyrics also touch on the complexities of love, as the narrator reflects on a fleeting encounter: "あのね、私あなたに会ったの 夢の中に置いてきたけどね" (You know, I met you, but I left you in my dreams). This line highlights the ephemeral nature of their connection, suggesting that even brief moments can leave a lasting impact. The narrator grapples with the intensity of their feelings, questioning, "どうして私が好きなの 一度しか会ったことがないのにね" (Why do I like you when we've only met once?). This introspection reveals the depth of emotion that can arise from even the most transient relationships.
The chorus reiterates the desire to sing about an unbreakable love, emphasizing the importance of memories and promises made. The lines, "壊(わす)れない愛を歌う 言葉を二人に課して" (We sing about an unbreakable love, binding words between us), suggest a commitment to preserving their love story through song, reinforcing the idea that love transcends time and distance.
In the latter part of the song, the narrator reflects on the passage of time and the enduring nature of love: "時代に乗って僕たちは 変わらず愛に生きるだろう" (Riding the times, we will continue to live in love). This acknowledgment of change, coupled with the assertion that their love will remain constant, encapsulates the song's overarching message about the resilience of love amidst life's inevitable transformations. The closing lines, "僕らが散って残るのは 変わらぬ愛の歌なんだろうな" (What remains after we scatter is the song of unchanging love), poignantly summarize the essence of the song, suggesting that while moments may fade, the love and memories endure.
